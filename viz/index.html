About

Where's the discretionary money going?

Each year the New York City Council designates hundreds of millions of dollars in discretionary funding — the "Schedule C" of the adopted budget — to community organizations, cultural institutions, senior centers, anti-violence programs, and more. In FY2027 that total is $655,764,999. This tool lets you explore it two ways: by category (what the money is for) and by administering agency (which City agency routes it), drilling down to the individual initiative.

Two lines are shown for each slice. Adopted is the discretionary total from the Council's adopted budget — the authoritative figure, reconciled to the dollar against the source document's printed totals. Itemized is the portion designated to a named recipient organization in the same document. The gap between them is money that flows through lump-sum or citywide initiatives (such as the Speaker's Initiative) with no organization-level breakout in the adopted schedule. Prototype note: the Itemized line depends on matching award records to initiative names and is materially complete only for recent years (roughly FY2023 onward); earlier years understate it. Always read the Adopted line as the reconciled truth.
